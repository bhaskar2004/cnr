What is Global Warming?

Global warming is the long-term heating of Earth's surface observed since the pre-industrial period due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning, which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth's atmosphere.

Critical Fact: Earth's average temperature has risen by 1.1¬∞C (2¬∞F) since the late 19th century, with most warming occurring in the past 40 years.

This temperature increase affects weather patterns, ice masses, sea levels, and ecosystems worldwide, presenting one of the most significant challenges of our time.

The Science Behind It

The Greenhouse Effect

Greenhouse gases in the atmosphere trap heat from the sun, creating a warming effect similar to a greenhouse. While this natural process is essential for life, human activities have intensified it dramatically.

Primary Causes

  • Fossil Fuel Combustion: Coal, oil, and natural gas burning for electricity, heat, and transportation releases massive amounts of CO‚ÇÇ
  • Deforestation: Clearing forests eliminates trees that absorb CO‚ÇÇ, while burning them releases stored carbon
  • Industrial Manufacturing: Cement production, chemical processing, and heavy industry emit significant greenhouse gases
  • Agriculture & Livestock: Cattle produce methane, while rice paddies and fertilizers release additional emissions
  • Transportation: Cars, trucks, ships, and planes burn petroleum products, contributing ~25% of global emissions

Environmental Impacts

Ice & Glaciers

Arctic sea ice is declining at 13% per decade. Major glaciers worldwide are retreating, threatening water supplies for millions.

Rising Sea Levels

Sea levels have risen 8-9 inches since 1880, with the rate of increase accelerating. Coastal communities face increasing flood risks.

Ocean Changes

Oceans absorb 90% of excess heat and 30% of CO‚ÇÇ, causing warming and acidification that threatens marine ecosystems and coral reefs.

Impact on Biodiversity

Climate change threatens up to one million species with extinction. Ecosystems are struggling to adapt to rapid temperature changes.

  • Habitat Loss: Animals lose their homes as environments change faster than they can adapt
  • Migration Disruption: Timing of seasons shifts, affecting migration and breeding patterns
  • Food Chain Collapse: Changes at one level affect entire ecosystems
  • Coral Bleaching: Rising ocean temperatures kill coral reefs, ocean biodiversity hotspots

Impact on Humanity

Health Effects

Heat waves, air pollution, and disease spread threaten human health worldwide. Climate change is already affecting vulnerable populations.

Food Security

Changing rainfall patterns, droughts, and extreme weather threaten crop yields and food production systems.

Water Scarcity

Over 2 billion people face water stress. Climate change intensifies droughts and affects freshwater availability.

Economic Cost: Climate change could cost the global economy trillions of dollars annually by 2050 if left unchecked.

Solutions & Innovation

Clean Energy Revolution

  • Solar Power: Rapidly becoming the cheapest source of electricity worldwide
  • Wind Energy: Onshore and offshore wind farms generating clean electricity
  • Hydroelectric: Harnessing water power for renewable energy
  • Nuclear Energy: Zero-emission baseload power generation
  • Battery Storage: Advanced technology storing renewable energy for when needed

Carbon Capture

Emerging technologies capture CO‚ÇÇ directly from the atmosphere or industrial sources, offering hope for reducing existing emissions.
